IUPAC Nomenclature

Alkynes

Alkynes are hydrocarbons that contain at least one carbon-carbon triple bond.Like alkenes, they are unsaturated compounds, meaning they have the capacity for additional reactions.
The general formula for alkynes is: CnH2n-2.


Key Information about Alkynes:

  1. Structural Formula: The presence of a triple bond gives alkynes the structure C≡C. This means there is one sigma (σ) bond and two pi (π) bonds between the carbon atoms.
  2. IUPAC Nomenclature: The nomenclature of alkynes follows similar principles as alkenes.
    1. Identify the longest continuous carbon chain containing the triple bond. This forms the parent chain.
    2. Number the carbon atoms in the chain, starting from the end closest to the triple bond, assigning the lowest possible numbers to the carbon atoms of the triple bond.
    3. Identify and name any alkyl groups (side chains) attached to the parent chain. Use prefixes like "methyl," "ethyl," etc.
    4. Combine the names of the substituents and the parent chain. Use hyphens to separate numbers and letters.
    5. Use the appropriate numerical prefix to indicate the position of the triple bond. Add the suffix "-yne" to indicate the presence of a triple bond.


Ball and Stick Pattern:

  1. Ethyne (Acetylene):
    • Chemical Formula: C2H2
    • Structure: H-C≡C-H
    • IUPAC Name: Ethyne

  2. Propyne:
    • Chemical Formula: C3H4
    • Structure: H-C≡C-CH3
    • IUPAC Name: Propyne

  3. Butyne (1-Butyne):
    • Chemical Formula: C4H6
    • Structure: H-C≡C-CH2-CH3
    • IUPAC Name: But-1-yne

  4. Pentyne (1-Pentyne):
    • Chemical Formula: C5H8
    • Structure: H-C≡C-CH2-CH2-CH3
    • IUPAC Name: Pent-1-yne
