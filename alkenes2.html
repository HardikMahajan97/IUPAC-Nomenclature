IUPAC Nomenclature

Alkenes

Alkenes are hydrocarbons that contain at least one carbon-carbon double bond. They are unsaturated compounds, meaning they have the capacity for additional reactions.
The general formula for alkenes is:  CnH2n.


Key Information about Alkenes:

  1. Structural Formula: Alkenes contain at least one carbon-carbon double bond, represented as C=C. This means there is one sigma (σ) bond and one pi (π) bond between the carbon atoms.
  2. IUPAC Nomenclature: The nomenclature of alkenes follows specific rules:
    1. Identify the longest continuous carbon chain containing the double bond. This forms the parent chain.
    2. Number the carbon atoms in the chain, starting from the end closest to the double bond, assigning the lowest possible numbers to the carbon atoms of the double bond.
    3. Identify and name any alkyl groups (side chains) attached to the parent chain. Use prefixes like "methyl," "ethyl," etc.
    4. Combine the names of the substituents and the parent chain. Use hyphens to separate numbers and letters.
    5. Use the appropriate numerical prefix to indicate the position of the double bond. Add the suffix "-ene" to indicate the presence of a double bond.

    • Structural Formula: H-C=C-CH2-CH3
    • IUPAC Name: But-1-ene



  4. 2-Butene (C4H8):
    • Structural Formula: CH3-C=C-CH2-CH3
    • IUPAC Name: But-2-ene
