IUPAC Nomenclature

Alkanes

Alkanes, also known as paraffins, are a class of organic compounds characterized by the presence of carbon (C) and hydrogen (H) atoms linked together by single covalent bonds.
They are considered the simplest type of hydrocarbons.

General Formula: CnH2n+2

Bonding: Each carbon atom forms four sigma bonds, resulting in a tetrahedral arrangement.

IUPAC Nomenclature of Alkanes:

The International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC) provides a standardized system for naming organic compounds. In alkanes, the names are based on the number of carbon atoms in the longest continuous chain.

Rules for Nomenclature

  • Parent Chain:

Longest Continuous Chain: Identify the longest continuous chain of carbon atoms. This chain gives the root name for the alkane.
Example: For the molecule CH₃-CH₂-CH₂-CH₃, the longest chain contains 4 carbons, so it's called butane.

  • Substituent:

Substituents Groups:Any branches or side chains attached to the main chain are treated as substituents.
Example: In CH₃-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-CH₃, the chain contains 4 carbons (butane) with a methyl (CH₃) group attached to the third carbon

  • Locating Substituents:

Numbering the Chain:Assign numbers to each carbon in the main chain, starting with the end closest to a substituent. The substituent gets the lowest possible number.
Example:For CH₃-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-CH₃, we number from the left. The methyl group is on the third carbon, so the full name is 3-methylbutane.

  • Naming Branched Alkanes:

Multiple Substituents:If there are multiple substituents, list them in alphabetical order, and indicate their position numbers.
Example:CH₃-CH(CH₃)-CH₂-CH₃ is 2,3-dimethylbutane.

  • Important Points:

  1. Hyphens and Commas: Use hyphens to separate words and commas between numbers.
  2. Alphabetical Order: When listing substituents, consider alphabetical order. For example, ethyl comes before methyl.
  3. Numbers:Numbers indicating substituent positions should be as low as possible.
  4. Common Names: Some simple alkanes have common names that are still widely used.
  5. IUPAC Prefixes:For very large or complex structures, prefixes like iso-, neo-, and sec- are used.

  • NOMENCLATURE:

  1. Methane (CH₄)
  2. Ethane (C₂H₆)
  3. Propane (C₃H₈)
  4. Butane (C₄H₁₀)
  5. Pentane (C₅H₁₂)


Some Examples:



Ball and Stick form:

Ball and Stick forms is a form of representing structure of a formula. In this form ball represents a Carbon and stick refers to the Hydrogen.


  1. CH₃-CH(CH₃)-CH₂-CH₂-CH₃: 3-Methylpentane
  2. CH₃-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-CH(CH₃)-CH₂-CH₃: 3,3-Dimethylhexane


Bond line form:

Bond line form is another way to represent a molecular formula.
Here every cut of intersection means presence of Carbon at that point. We don't represent Hydrogen in Bond Line form.



  1. CH₃-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-CH₃: 3-Ethyl-2,3-dimethylhexane
  2. CH₃-CH₂-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-CH(CH₃)-CH₂-CH₂-CH₃: 4-Ethyl-3,3-dimethylheptane
